Our Story

Made in Umbria
with Love

A family, a grove, and an obsession with making truly great olive oil.

How It Began

Two People.
One Grove.

When Marco and Amanda left their careers in the city to take over Marco's family property in the hills above Spoleto, their friends thought they were mad. The farmhouse needed work, the grove was overgrown, and neither of them knew much about olive oil beyond the bottle they kept by the stove.

But they had something that can't be bought: a deep connection to the land and the stubbornness to learn everything from scratch. They spent two years restoring the trees, studying with local agronomists, and visiting mills and producers across Umbria and Tuscany.

Their first real harvest in 2014 produced just 80 liters. Friends and family tasted it and said it was the best olive oil they'd ever had. Olivando was born.

The Grove

500 Trees,
One Vision

Our grove sits at 400 meters above sea level in the Valnerina valley of Umbria — a landscape of ancient olive trees, wild herbs, and clean mountain air. The soil is rich in limestone and clay, which gives our oils their distinctive mineral character.

We farm around 500 trees, a mix of the classic Umbrian varieties: Moraiolo, Frantoio, and Leccino. Each has its own personality — Moraiolo gives intensity and bitterness, Frantoio adds floral notes, and Leccino brings softness and balance.

We farm without synthetic pesticides or herbicides, relying instead on traditional pruning, cover crops, and careful observation of the land. Not because it's a marketing point — because it's the right way.

What We Stand For

Our Principles

01
Radical Transparency

We tell you exactly which grove, which variety, which harvest, and which mill produced the oil in your bottle. No blending, no hiding, no vague labels.

02
Obsessive Freshness

Olive oil is a fresh product. We bottle to order, store in nitrogen-flushed tanks, and ship with minimal delay so you get oil as close to harvest day as possible.

03
Sustainable Farming

Low-impact cultivation means healthier trees, healthier soil, and better tasting oil. We view sustainability not as a cost but as the foundation of quality.
